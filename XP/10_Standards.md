# Standards

## Project

IACodeEditors

**Practice:** Standards

**Owner (Accountable):** Daniel Nuñez

**Authors:** Daniel Nuñez, Ian (AI Collaborator)

---

# Purpose

Establish shared ways of working that simplify collaboration,
reduce unnecessary decisions,
and allow participants to focus on solving meaningful problems.

Standards exist to make development easier,
not more restrictive.

---

# Philosophy

A standard is successful when people naturally want to follow it.

Standards should simplify work rather than introduce unnecessary
complexity.

The project values standards that enable collaboration,
consistency,
and continuous learning.

---

# Purpose Before Standards

Standards are never created in isolation.

They emerge from understanding the project's purpose.

Purpose

↓

Understanding

↓

Experience

↓

Standard

↓

Consistency

↓

Better Outcomes

A standard should always support the project's current objectives.

---

# Standards Evolve

Standards are not permanent.

As understanding improves,
standards should also improve.

A standard remains valuable only while it continues helping the
project.

Continuous improvement applies equally to standards.

---

# Reducing Cognitive Load

Standards eliminate repetitive decisions.

Examples include:

- document structure
- terminology
- architectural conventions
- workflow organization
- naming conventions
- project practices

Reducing routine decisions allows participants to dedicate more energy
to solving new problems.

---

# Consistency Enables Collaboration

Shared standards create predictable ways of working.

Consistency makes artifacts easier to:

- understand
- improve
- maintain
- review
- reuse

Consistency strengthens collaboration without limiting creativity.

---

# Standards Support Onboarding

Standards accelerate onboarding.

New contributors should quickly understand:

- how the project is organized
- how documentation is written
- how workflows operate
- how practices are applied
- how decisions are documented

The project teaches itself through its standards.

---

# Creativity Through Standards

Standards do not reduce creativity.

They protect it.

Routine activities become predictable.

Creative effort remains focused on discovering better solutions.

---

# AI and Standards

Artificial Intelligence contributes by:

- promoting consistency
- identifying deviations
- refining terminology
- improving documentation
- suggesting simplifications
- reinforcing shared practices

AI helps maintain standards.

Human participants decide when standards should evolve.

---

Throughout the development of IACodeEditors,
standards naturally emerged from repeated collaboration.

Document structures,
practice templates,

# Future Evolution

Standards should continue evolving as the project grows.

Every contributor is encouraged to:

- simplify existing standards

---

# Key Insight

Standards exist to simplify collaboration.

Their value is not measured by how strictly they are enforced,
but by how effectively they help people solve problems together.

A good standard is one that people naturally follow because it makes
their work easier,
supports the project's purpose,
and continuously evolves as understanding grows.
