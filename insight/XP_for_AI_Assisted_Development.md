# Extreme Programming for AI-Assisted Development

> Working title for an ongoing research journal.

## Abstract

Artificial Intelligence is changing how software is developed.

This research does not begin by proposing a new methodology.

Instead, it observes the application of Extreme Programming while building the **IACodeEditors** project and records the lessons learned.

The objective is to understand how XP behaves when software is developed collaboratively by humans and AI assistants.

---

# Research Question

How can Extreme Programming be effectively applied in AI-assisted software development while preserving its original values and principles?

---

# Scope

The research focuses on:

- practical observations
- experiments
- successes
- failures
- emerging practices

The research does not aim to redefine XP, but to understand its application in a new technological context.

---

# Evidence Source

The primary source of evidence is the **IACodeEditors** project.

Every significant architectural or process decision may contribute evidence.

# Initial Hypotheses

- XP values remain valid in AI-assisted development.
- Some XP practices may require reinterpretation rather than replacement.
- Pair Programming can naturally evolve into Human–AI Pair Programming.
- Collective Ownership extends beyond source code into shared knowledge.
- AI can strengthen feedback cycles without replacing customer collaboration.
