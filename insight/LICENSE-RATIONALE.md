# License Rationale

## Purpose

This document explains **why** the project adopts the GNU General Public License v3 (GPLv3).

It is not intended to be a legal interpretation of the license. Instead, it records the architectural and strategic reasoning behind this decision.

---

# Why GPLv3?

The primary goal of **IACodeEditors** is not to commercialize software licenses.

Its main objective is to create, validate and share knowledge about AI-assisted software development through the construction of a real software project.

The project seeks to become a collaborative learning initiative where ideas, implementations and improvements remain available to the community.

GPLv3 best supports this objective.

---

# Guiding Principles

## Knowledge over Ownership

The greatest value of this project lies in the knowledge generated during its evolution rather than in exclusive ownership of its source code.

## Community Improvement

Contributions from the community are considered an essential part of the project's evolution.

Keeping improvements available benefits everyone participating in the ecosystem.

## Collective Learning

The project follows the spirit of collaborative software development.

Knowledge should be shared, discussed, challenged and continuously improved.

## Transparency

The complete implementation should remain available for study, experimentation and learning.

---

# Why not a more permissive license?

Permissive licenses such as MIT or Apache 2.0 are excellent choices for many projects.

However, they also allow third parties to incorporate the software into proprietary products without contributing improvements back to the community.

For the current goals of IACodeEditors, preserving the collaborative evolution of the project is considered more valuable than maximizing unrestricted commercial adoption.

This decision is specific to this project and should not be interpreted as a general preference for GPL over other licenses.

---

# Future Perspective

Future projects may adopt different licenses according to their own objectives.

Examples include:

- Apache 2.0 for reusable infrastructure components.
- MIT for educational examples or lightweight libraries.
- GPLv3 for collaborative knowledge-driven initiatives.

The license should always emerge from the purpose of the project rather than from convention.

---

# Design Principle

> Architectural, technical and licensing decisions should emerge from the purpose of the project and the experience gained during its development, not from trends or conventions adopted without context.
